# Ideal Customer Profile

## Who We're Looking For

Organizations where the narrative stakes are real — reputation risk, public scrutiny, political pressure, regulatory exposure, or a story the world is already telling about them (accurately or not). These orgs need media that moves people, not just content that checks a box. They have budget, urgency, and something worth saying.

## Qualifying Signals

The agent should prioritize opportunities that match one or more of these triggers:

- **Rebrand or repositioning** — the org is changing how it presents itself publicly
- **Launch or campaign** — new initiative, program, product, or public awareness push
- **Funding or grant** — new money allocated for communications, outreach, or media
- **Crisis or controversy** — negative press, public backlash, regulatory action, whistleblower events
- **Growth outpacing brand** — expanding fast but public perception hasn't caught up
- **Competitor gaining ground** — losing the narrative to a rival or alternative
- **Leadership change** — new comms director, CMO, or public affairs lead who'll want to make their mark
- **Wasted budget signal** — evidence of previous media spend that didn't land (stale YouTube channels, low-engagement campaigns, generic stock-photo content)
- **Federal RFP** — active solicitation for video production, media, communications, or creative services

## Disqualifying Signals

Skip these — they waste pipeline:

- Orgs with no communications budget (small local nonprofits under $1M revenue)
- Pure brand/design agencies looking to subcontract commodity video work
- "Content mill" requests — social media clips, talking head testimonials, event highlight reels
- Orgs in industries with no narrative stakes (routine B2B SaaS, consumer products, retail)
- Anything where the ask is clearly under $15K

## Priority Verticals

### Tier 1 — Active Pipeline
- **Government / Public Affairs** — state agencies, city/county government, tribal nations and indigenous organizations. Arizona is home turf but national is fine. Border/immigration is ground zero for narrative stakes.
- **Healthcare Systems** — Banner, HonorHealth, Mayo Clinic Scottsdale, AHCCCS, community health orgs. Patient stories, public health campaigns, recruitment.

### Tier 2 — High Potential
- **Defense & Aerospace** — Raytheon, Boeing Mesa, Luke AFB, Honeywell Aerospace. Recruitment, public affairs, community relations. Long sales cycles but big contracts.
- **Technology & Manufacturing** — TSMC Arizona, Intel Chandler. Historic investment in AZ, massive workforce buildout, community narrative challenges.
- **International NGOs** — organizations operating in contested or sensitive environments. Documentary-grade storytelling for advocacy, fundraising, or awareness.

### Tier 3 — Opportunistic
- **Higher Education** — ASU, NAU, UA system. Enrollment marketing, research storytelling, capital campaign media.
- **Energy / Utilities** — APS, SRP, renewable energy projects with community opposition or public education needs.
- **Real Estate / Development** — large mixed-use or controversial developments where public narrative matters.

## Target Titles

Decision makers who control or influence media/communications budget:

### Primary (highest priority)
- Director of Communications
- Chief Communications Officer (CCO)
- VP of Marketing / VP of Communications
- Public Affairs Director / Public Information Officer
- Director of Strategic Communications

### Secondary (influencers or budget holders)
- Chief of Staff
- Deputy Director (of relevant department)
- Marketing Director
- Community Engagement Director
- Government Affairs Director

### Hold for Review (don't auto-send)
- C-suite (CEO, Governor, Mayor, President) — unless they have a comms qualifier in their title
- Board members
- Elected officials

## Org Size & Budget

- **Minimum org size**: 50+ employees or $5M+ annual revenue/budget
- **Target contract range**: $25K–$150K per project, $8K–$20K/month retainer
- **Sweet spot**: $50K+ one-off or $10K+/month retainer
- **Floor**: Don't pursue anything likely under $15K total

## Geographic Fit

- **Arizona first** — Scottsdale, Phoenix metro, Tucson, tribal lands. Local relationships compound.
- **National is fine** for government, nonprofit, healthcare, and defense — especially if the work involves Arizona or the Southwest.
- **International** only for NGOs or documentary work with real narrative stakes.
